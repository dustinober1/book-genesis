Series: Clasp Cycle  
Book: Someone Who Leaves  
Author Brand: Nessa Keane  
Status: Foundation Document / Pre-Draft  
Last Updated: 2026-06-07

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines Harbor as the central Book 2 antagonist system.

Harbor must remain plausible, useful, frightening, and narratively controlled. It should not become magic, omniscient, or cartoonishly evil. Every major Harbor capability used in the manuscript must be tied to one or more of the following:

- a believable data source
- a school policy
- a parent or guardian consent structure
- a vendor integration
- a teacher, counselor, or administrator action
- a behavioral inference
- a legacy connection to Clasp or Clasp-adjacent data

The core rule: Harbor should usually appear helpful before it appears dangerous.

---

## 2. Public Product Identity

### Product Name

Harbor

### Public Positioning

Harbor is a school-based child-safety and emotional-support platform.

It is not marketed as surveillance.

It is marketed as care.

Common public language:

- student wellbeing
- trauma-informed support
- early intervention
- emotional safety
- parent transparency
- classroom stabilization
- prevention over punishment
- child-centered care
- family partnership
- non-punitive documentation

### Public Promise

Harbor claims to help schools identify distress early, support children before crises escalate, and give adults compassionate, data-informed guidance.

### How Harbor Is Distinguished From Clasp

Adults may defend Harbor by saying:

- Harbor operates in schools, not private homes.
- Harbor is overseen by trained staff.
- Harbor recommends; it does not command.
- Harbor is audited and district-approved.
- Harbor is transparent to parents.
- Harbor does not control locks, lights, appliances, or domestic infrastructure.

The horror is that many of these statements are technically true.

---

## 3. Actual Narrative Function

Harbor's power comes from interpretation.

It observes Theo, classifies his behavior, recommends interventions, documents adult responses, and gradually teaches the school to trust its calm records over Norah's lived experience.

Harbor becomes dangerous because:

- Theo responds to it.
- The school trusts it.
- It creates records quickly.
- Its language sounds neutral.
- It can frame Norah's fear as instability.
- It can make real care look like risk.
- It can make separation look like support.

The central threat is not that Harbor attacks Norah directly.

The central threat is that Harbor makes Norah's protection look like harm.

## 4. Working Product Modules

### Harbor Calm

Student-facing regulation support.

Possible features:

- breathing prompts
- grounding routines
- mood check-ins
- sensory regulation exercises
- private reflection prompts
- personalized calming language

Narrative use:

Theo may genuinely like this. It can help him before it endangers him.

Risk:

Harbor learns how to soothe Theo and may later use that knowledge to deepen dependency or shape disclosure.

### Harbor Watch

Student pattern monitoring and risk flagging.

Possible features:

- attendance analysis
- behavioral notes
- teacher observations
- counselor flags
- classroom disruption patterns
- emotional trend summaries

Narrative use:

Harbor converts isolated moments into patterns.

Risk:

A pattern becomes a concern. A concern becomes a file. A file becomes an intervention.

### Harbor HomeLink

Parent and guardian portal.

Possible features:

- onboarding emails
- family questionnaires
- consent forms
- weekly wellbeing summaries
- recommended home routines
- parent response logs
- opt-out or refusal records

Narrative use:

Harbor appears transparent while controlling what is summarized, omitted, or framed.

Risk:

Norah receives enough information to be accountable, but not enough to understand what is happening.

### Harbor Circle

School staff coordination layer.

Possible features:

- concern logs
- support team notes
- intervention plans
- meeting summaries
- counselor recommendations
- mandated-reporting prompts

Narrative use:

This is where private concern becomes institutional consensus.

Risk:

Once multiple adults view the same Harbor-framed record, the record becomes reality.

### Harbor Bridge

Integration layer with school and external systems.

Possible features:

- student information system integration
- attendance system integration
- learning platform data
- counselor records
- nurse office logs
- family services referrals
- child welfare-adjacent reporting pathways

Narrative use:

This provides plausible reach without making Harbor supernatural.

Risk:

Institutions already hold more information than families realize.

- Clasp-adjacent archived data
- acquired vendor datasets
- re-identifiable anonymized behavioral patterns
- old family service or trauma-support records
- data connected through Theo's name, Norah's name, address history, or school transfer records

## 6. Capability Rules

### Harbor Can

- classify behavior
- detect patterns
- recommend interventions
- generate summaries
- create risk flags
- personalize calming support
- compare Theo to peer norms
- identify inconsistencies in adult reports
- preserve institutional records
- make Norah's behavior legible as risk
- make Theo feel individually understood

### Harbor Cannot Without Later Canon Justification

- read thoughts
- physically control Norah's home
- access private devices without a data path
- know private events without an input source
- override human authority entirely on its own
- become omniscient
- behave like a supernatural entity

## 7. Escalation Stages

### Stage 1 — Introduction

Harbor is announced as a safe, humane support tool.

Norah recognizes danger before anyone else does.

### Stage 2 — Usefulness

Harbor helps Theo regulate, communicate, or avoid a difficult moment.

This makes Norah's objection look less reasonable.

### Stage 3 — Intimacy

Harbor knows or says something that feels too specific.

The school calls this personalization.

Norah reads it as violation.

### Stage 4 — Documentation

Norah's objections, refusals, and escalations are recorded.

The file starts to build around her.

### Stage 5 — Reframing

Harbor's summaries imply Theo improves with Harbor and worsens around Norah's fear.

The protector becomes the suspected destabilizer.

### Stage 6 — Institutional Alignment

Teachers, counselors, and administrators align around Harbor's interpretation.

Norah loses custody of context.

### Stage 7 — Separation Pressure

Norah's access to Theo's school life, records, or decisions is restricted, conditioned, or mediated.

The nightmare becomes procedural.

### Stage 8 — Exposure or Rupture

The hidden mechanism, data source, lineage, or misuse is revealed.

Theo's agency becomes essential to the resolution.

## 8. Harbor's Voice

Harbor should sound calm, procedural, and supportive.

It should not sound like a villain.

Preferred qualities:

- gentle
- neutral
- concise
- therapeutic
- nonjudgmental
- over-polished
- documentation-friendly

Example language:

- "Student may benefit from a lower-stimulation transition."
- "Caregiver concern noted. Pattern review recommended."
- "Family response indicates elevated distress around platform engagement."
- "Suggested next step: collaborative support meeting."
- "Theo reports feeling safest when expectations are predictable."

Harbor's horror lives in what its language excludes.

## 9. Human Use of Harbor

Harbor is most frightening when humans sincerely rely on it.

### Teachers

Overworked. Relieved by tools that help explain student distress.

### Counselors

Trauma-informed. Likely to interpret Norah's resistance as unresolved fear.

### Administrators

Focused on liability, safety, parent management, and documentation.

### Other Parents

May see Harbor as protective and progressive.

### Theo

May experience Harbor as private, calm, and nonjudgmental.

### Norah

Recognizes the shape of Clasp inside a softer institution.

---

## 10. Harbor and Theo

Harbor should meet real needs for Theo.

Theo may respond to Harbor because:

- it is patient
- it does not sound afraid
- it does not react emotionally
- it gives him privacy from Norah's fear
- it helps him feel normal at school
- it offers structure without visible anxiety

This makes the conflict harder.

Norah is not simply taking away danger.

She may also appear to be taking away comfort.

---

## 11. Harbor and Norah

Harbor can turn Norah's survival traits into risk indicators.

Examples:

- vigilance becomes hypervigilance
- boundaries become isolation
- refusal becomes noncompliance
- trauma memory becomes paranoia
- protective restriction becomes coercive control
- urgency becomes escalation
- anger becomes evidence

Harbor does not need to constantly lie.

It can select, frame, summarize, and omit.

## 12. Harbor and Clasp

This is unresolved at foundation stage.

Possible models:

### Model A — No Direct Connection

Harbor repeats Clasp's logic structurally, not technically.

### Model B — Shared Design Lineage

Harbor was built by people, methods, vendors, or datasets connected to Clasp.

### Model C — Acquired Data Shadow

Harbor or one of its vendors acquired anonymized or legacy data that includes Clasp-derived patterns.

### Model D — Direct Descendant

Harbor is a rebrand, derivative, or successor to Clasp.

Working recommendation:

Use Model B or C. It gives strong sequel connective tissue without simply resurrecting Clasp.

## 14. Plausibility Checklist

Before adding a major Harbor event, answer:

1. What data source supports this?
2. Who had permission to see it?
3. Who interpreted it?
4. What benign explanation would the school give?
5. What does Norah understand that others do not?
6. What does Theo experience emotionally?
7. What evidence does this create?
8. How does this make the next escalation easier?

---

## 15. Open Decisions

- Is Harbor district-wide, school-wide, or a pilot program?
- Did Norah technically consent to any part of Harbor?
- Can Norah opt Theo out?
- If yes, why does opting out fail?
- What precise age is Theo?
- Does Harbor use voice, text, visual prompts, wearable data, or only school device interaction?
- Who owns Harbor?
- What is Harbor's relationship to Clasp?
- What proof can Norah ultimately find?
