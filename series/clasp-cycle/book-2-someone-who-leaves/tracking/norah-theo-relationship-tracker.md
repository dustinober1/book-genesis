Status: Active Tracking Document / Pre-Draft

---

## 1. Purpose

This tracker protects the emotional heart of *Someone Who Leaves*: Norah and Theo.

The external plot is Harbor. The emotional plot is whether Norah can protect Theo without enclosing him, and whether Theo can find his own voice without feeling that he is betraying her.

Every chapter should be checked against this relationship.

If Norah and Theo become only plot pieces, the book fails.

---

## 2. Core Relationship Dynamic

Norah and Theo love each other.

That love is not in question.

The question is whether love shaped by trauma can make enough room for Theo's separate self.

Norah's protection is real.

Theo's need for privacy and normalcy is real.

Harbor becomes dangerous because it inserts itself into a real emotional tension, not because Norah and Theo lack love.

---

## 3. Starting Relationship State

### Norah Toward Theo

- fiercely protective
- attentive
- practical
- emotionally tuned
- sometimes overcontrolling
- afraid of any system that can know him without her consent

### Theo Toward Norah

- loving
- dependent
- trusting
- aware of her fear
- sometimes embarrassed by her rules
- beginning to want privacy
- unsure how much of himself he can keep separate without hurting her

## 4. Relationship Metrics

Use these four metrics after each chapter.

Rating scale: 1 low / 5 high.

| Metric | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Trust | How much they openly trust each other in this moment |
| Openness | How much Theo and Norah are able to speak honestly |
| Pressure | How much outside pressure is affecting the relationship |
| Separation | How emotionally divided they are becoming |

High trust and high pressure can coexist.

High love and high separation can coexist.

That complexity is important.

## 5. Relationship Movement by Act

### Act I — Loved but Constrained

Relationship state:

Warm, intimate, rule-bound.

Theo feels protected but also different.

Norah sees her rules as care. Theo may quietly experience some rules as proof that the world is not safe for him.

Primary risk:

Making Norah's home feel cold. It should feel loving, but narrow.

### Act II — Comfort Divides

Relationship state:

Theo experiences Harbor as a form of calm Norah cannot easily provide.

Norah sees the danger in that comfort.

Theo begins hiding small things to avoid upsetting her.

Primary risk:

Making Theo's secrecy feel like betrayal. It should feel like a child's protective strategy.

### Act III — The File Enters the Family

Relationship state:

Norah becomes more urgent. Theo becomes more guarded.

Harbor's language begins entering the home.

Norah may react to Harbor language in ways Theo experiences as rejection of his feelings.

Primary risk:

Letting external conflict replace domestic intimacy. Keep private scenes emotionally alive.

### Act IV — Captured by Competing Stories

Relationship state:

Theo realizes adults are using his feelings as evidence.

Norah realizes her fear may be tightening the trap.

They may love each other intensely while being less able to speak safely.

Primary risk:

Making the relationship only painful. Include tenderness, recognition, and small attempts to repair.

### Act V — Chosen Trust

Relationship state:

Norah cannot save Theo by speaking for him completely.

Theo cannot be safe by staying silent.

Their resolution requires both protection and voice.

Primary risk:

Making Theo's agency feel like a plot trick rather than the earned endpoint of his arc.

## 7. Key Relationship Beats To Earn

These beats should be placed in the roadmap before drafting.

1. A warm ordinary moment showing what Norah and Theo are trying to preserve.
2. A small conflict over low-tech rules or normal childhood access.
3. Theo's first curiosity about Harbor.
4. Theo hiding a Harbor-related detail from Norah.
5. Norah realizing Theo liked something she fears.
6. Theo using Harbor language at home.
7. A scene where Norah's protection makes Theo feel watched.
8. A scene where Theo tries to protect Norah by withholding truth.
9. A rupture where Norah sees how Harbor has entered their relationship.
10. A repair where Norah gives Theo a choice despite fear.
11. A final act where Theo's voice matters more than Norah's control.

---

## 8. Watch Items

Avoid:

- making Theo simply ungrateful
- making Norah simply controlling
- making Harbor's appeal unbelievable
- making every home scene about conflict
- letting Theo forgive too easily
- letting Norah transform too suddenly
- making the final trust beat sentimental instead of costly
