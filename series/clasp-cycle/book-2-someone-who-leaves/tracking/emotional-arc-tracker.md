## 1. Purpose

This tracker records the emotional movement of *Someone Who Leaves* across the full manuscript.

The plot can escalate through Harbor, school pressure, and evidence, but the book only works if the emotional arc remains clear:

Norah must move from protection through control and desperation into chosen trust.

Theo must move from dependence through secrecy and divided loyalty into chosen voice.

The ending should resolve both movements together.

---

## 2. Core Emotional Spine

### Norah

Protection → Control → Desperation → Exposure → Surrender → Chosen Trust

### Theo

Dependence → Curiosity → Secrecy → Attachment → Divided Loyalty → Self-Protection → Chosen Voice

### Relationship

Warm constraint → private friction → divided comfort → documented strain → procedural capture → risky repair → trust with agency

## 5. Emotional Beats To Preserve

These beats should be earned across the roadmap and draft.

1. Norah's home feels loving, not cold.
2. Theo wants normalcy before Harbor fully arrives.
3. Harbor helps Theo in a way Norah cannot dismiss.
4. Theo hides something to protect Norah from her own fear.
5. Norah reacts to Harbor's intimacy and accidentally increases Theo's shame.
6. A school record makes Norah's love look like instability.
7. Theo realizes adults are using his feelings as evidence.
8. Norah realizes control is helping Harbor's argument.
9. Theo contributes truth Norah cannot manufacture.
10. Norah chooses trust without becoming naive.

---

## 6. Emotional Risk Watch

Avoid:

- making Norah simply wrong
- making Theo simply right
- making Harbor only sinister
- making emotional repair too easy
- making the ending solve trauma completely
- letting institutional plot overwhelm parent-child intimacy
- using repeated panic beats without progression
- resolving Theo's secrecy through one clean confession
