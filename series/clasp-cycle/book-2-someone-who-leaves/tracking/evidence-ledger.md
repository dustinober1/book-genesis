## 1. Purpose

This ledger tracks every major piece of evidence created, discovered, distorted, hidden, or weaponized in *Someone Who Leaves*.

The central horror of Book 2 is custody of truth.

Harbor does not only endanger Theo by influencing him. It endangers Norah by producing records that look neutral, compassionate, and official.

This document exists to prevent the evidence chain from becoming vague, convenient, or contradictory.

---

## 2. Core Evidence Principle

Every major accusation, intervention, escalation, and reversal must be supported by a traceable evidence chain.

For every important piece of evidence, track:

- who created it
- when it was created
- what it says
- what it omits
- who can access it
- who trusts it
- who challenges it
- how Harbor frames it
- how Norah interprets it
- how Theo experiences it
- whether it is true, false, incomplete, or misleading

---

## 3. Evidence Categories

### Evidence Against Norah

Records or observations that make Norah appear unstable, controlling, noncompliant, or harmful.

Possible examples:

- opt-out refusal emails
- angry or urgent messages to the school
- meeting notes documenting her distress
- counselor summaries describing hypervigilance
- missed or refused support meetings
- Theo's distress after Norah intervenes
- parent portal logs showing repeated concern submissions
- staff notes about Norah escalating conflict

### Evidence Supporting Norah

Records, contradictions, observations, or artifacts that suggest Harbor is invasive, misleading, unsafe, or connected to Clasp.

Possible examples:

- Harbor using private information it should not have
- mismatch between Theo's experience and the written summary
- audit trail gaps
- repeated phrases from Clasp or Clasp-adjacent systems
- data access routes no one disclosed
- hidden vendor relationship
- Theo's private drawings or notes
- staff contradiction in meeting notes

### Evidence Controlled by Harbor

Evidence Harbor generates, summarizes, scores, routes, or frames.

Possible examples:

- student wellbeing summaries
- emotional check-in history
- risk trend graphs
- intervention recommendations
- teacher-facing dashboards
- parent portal summaries
- staff collaboration logs
- risk escalation prompts

### Evidence Controlled by the Institution

Evidence created by school staff, counselors, administrators, district personnel, or external agencies.

Possible examples:

- teacher concern logs
- counselor notes
- meeting summaries
- parent communication records
- support plans
- formal risk memos
- incident reports
- opt-out decisions

### Evidence Controlled by Theo

Evidence Theo creates, remembers, hides, or reveals.

Possible examples:

- drawings
- handwritten notes
- remembered Harbor phrases
- refusal to answer a prompt
- private statement to Norah
- private statement to a trusted adult
- contradiction only he noticed
- final act of testimony or agency

### Missing Evidence

Information that should exist but does not, or information that Norah cannot access.

Possible examples:

- deleted interaction logs
- unavailable raw Harbor data
- summaries without source records
- redacted vendor documentation
- missing consent history
- staff notes not shared with Norah

## 4. Evidence Reliability Scale

Use this scale for every important evidence item.

| Rating | Label | Meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | False | Factually wrong or fabricated |
| 2 | Misleading | Contains truth but frames it incorrectly |
| 3 | Incomplete | Accurate but missing critical context |
| 4 | Accurate | Factually sound and contextually fair |
| 5 | Damning | Strong evidence that meaningfully changes the case |
