---

## 1. Purpose

This tracker records how the school, district, counselors, staff, parents, and external systems gradually increase pressure on Norah.

The institutional horror of *Someone Who Leaves* depends on escalation that feels procedural, not cartoonish.

No single adult needs to be evil.

The danger comes from aligned incentives, liability language, documentation, concern, and trusted systems.

---

## 2. Core Principle

The institution should always have a reasonable explanation for its next step.

That does not make the step harmless.

For each institutional escalation, track:

- the official reason
- the hidden or emotional consequence
- who initiates it
- who benefits from it
- what document it creates
- how it affects Norah's credibility
- how it affects Theo's agency
- how it strengthens Harbor's authority

---

## 3. Institutional Pressure Levels

| Level | Label | Description |
|---:|---|---|
| 0 | No concern | Norah and Theo are not under institutional scrutiny |
| 1 | Routine onboarding | Harbor is introduced through normal school channels |
| 2 | Parent concern logged | Norah's objections are recorded as engagement data |
| 3 | Staff discussion | Teachers/counselors discuss Theo and Norah internally |
| 4 | Support plan | School creates formal recommendations or interventions |
| 5 | Risk framing | Norah is named or implied as part of Theo's distress pattern |
| 6 | Access mediation | Norah's communication or records access is managed/limited |
| 7 | External escalation | Outside services, district review, or mandated-reporting-adjacent pathways appear |
| 8 | Procedural separation | The system creates real separation pressure between Norah and Theo |

## 5. Provisional Institutional Escalation Map

### Act I — Routine Adoption

Likely levels:

- Level 1
- Level 2

Events:

- Harbor rollout announcement
- parent packet or meeting
- Norah's first concern
- school reassurance
- concern logged as engagement

Key danger:

Norah's objection enters the record before anyone accepts that Harbor is dangerous.

### Act II — Staff Confidence

Likely levels:

- Level 2
- Level 3
- early Level 4

Events:

- Harbor success with Theo
- staff praise
- counselor involvement
- suggested home routines
- Norah asks for opt-out or data access

Key danger:

Harbor gains institutional credibility by helping Theo.

### Act III — Family Concern

Likely levels:

- Level 4
- Level 5

Events:

- support plan
- family meeting
- Norah's past trauma becomes part of interpretation
- Theo's distress is linked to home conflict
- school begins documenting Norah as part of pattern

Key danger:

The school stops treating Norah as a worried parent and begins treating her as a variable.

### Act IV — Managed Parent

Likely levels:

- Level 6
- Level 7
- possible Level 8

Events:

- mediated communication
- limited direct contact with staff
- restricted records or delayed disclosure
- formal risk review
- external referral or mandated-reporting-adjacent pressure

Key danger:

Norah cannot directly correct the record anymore.

### Act V — Rupture

Likely levels:

- Level 7
- Level 8 challenged or reversed

Events:

- proof disrupts institutional story
- Harbor's reliability is questioned
- school/district enters damage control
- Theo's agency breaks the frame

Key danger:

Even when exposed, the institution may protect itself before it protects Norah and Theo.

## 6. Institutional Actors To Define

These roles need named characters or clear functions before chapter drafting.

| Role | Function | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Theo's teacher | Daily observations; first adult to praise Harbor | Can sincerely trust the tool |
| School counselor | Trauma-informed interpretation; family meetings | Can pathologize Norah gently |
| Principal/administrator | Liability, parent management, policy enforcement | Can proceduralize harm |
| District Harbor lead | Rollout defender; compliance voice | Can depersonalize concerns |
| Other parent | Social pressure; normalizes Harbor | Can isolate Norah socially |
| Possible ally | Teacher, tech staff, counselor, parent, or journalist | Must not solve too much too early |

---

## 7. Watch Items

Avoid:

- school staff acting like obvious villains
- immediate extreme escalation without paperwork trail
- vague references to legal danger without specific documents
- repeating the same meeting beat
- letting institution replace Harbor as the only threat
- making everyone except Norah foolish

The most frightening version is one where several intelligent, caring people make individually defensible choices that collectively trap her.
