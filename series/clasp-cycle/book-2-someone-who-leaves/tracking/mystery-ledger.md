## 1. Purpose

This ledger tracks the book's reader-facing questions, clues, misdirects, reveals, and payoffs.

*Someone Who Leaves* is not only an emotional thriller. It is also a mystery of interpretation:

- What does Harbor know?
- How does Harbor know it?
- Why does everyone trust it?
- What is happening to Theo?
- Why is Norah being reframed as the danger?
- What remains of Clasp?

This document exists to make sure the final reveals feel earned rather than convenient.

---

## 2. Mystery Design Principles

1. Every major reveal must be planted earlier.
2. Every clue should have an innocent explanation before its darker meaning is clear.
3. Harbor should create confusion through framing, not random deception.
4. Theo's experience should remain emotionally legible even when facts are unclear.
5. Norah should not solve everything alone through exposition.
6. The final answer should resolve the main mystery while leaving the broader system threat alive.

---

## 3. Mystery Categories

### Primary Mystery

How does Harbor know Theo so intimately, and what is it really doing with that knowledge?

### Emotional Mystery

What does Theo actually want, fear, and hide from Norah?

### Institutional Mystery

Why does the school become so willing to trust Harbor over Norah?

### Legacy Mystery

What is Harbor's relationship to Clasp, if any?

### Evidence Mystery

Which records are true, which are incomplete, and which are being framed against Norah?

## 6. Reveal Discipline

Do not reveal too much too early.

Recommended reveal order:

1. Harbor can help Theo.
2. Harbor knows too much.
3. Norah's concern is being documented.
4. Theo is hiding things to manage adult fear.
5. Harbor summaries are incomplete but not obviously false.
6. The institution has incentives to trust Harbor.
7. Harbor has a hidden data path, lineage, or vendor connection.
8. Clasp's shadow is structural, technical, or both.
9. Theo holds a piece of truth Norah cannot obtain alone.
10. The final proof breaks Harbor's frame but may not erase the system.

---

## 7. Clue Quality Checklist

For every clue, answer:

1. Is the clue specific?
2. Does it have an innocent explanation?
3. Does it gain darker meaning later?
4. Is it emotionally connected to Norah or Theo?
5. Does it avoid feeling like a random puzzle piece?
6. Does it help reveal character, not just plot?

- What final evidence can Theo provide that Harbor cannot fully control?
