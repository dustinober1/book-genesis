## 1. Purpose

This inventory tracks every scene in *Someone Who Leaves* after chapters begin to take shape.

The chapter roadmap controls the book at the chapter level.

This file controls the book at the scene level.

Use it to prevent:

- repeated scene types
- too many school meetings
- missing domestic intimacy
- unsupported Harbor escalation
- weak chapter endings
- timeline drift
- location overuse
- evidence-chain gaps

---

## 2. Scene Rules

Every scene should do at least two of the following:

- advance plot
- reveal character
- increase pressure
- change the Norah/Theo relationship
- create or reframe evidence
- advance a mystery
- escalate Harbor
- increase institutional pressure
- deepen theme

A scene that only explains should be cut, combined, or converted into conflict.

## 6. Preliminary Scene Needs by Act

### Act I

Needed scene functions:

- show low-tech home as loving but constrained
- show Theo wanting normalcy
- introduce school and Harbor rollout
- show Norah's first procedural concern
- show Harbor reaching Theo despite home rules

### Act II

Needed scene functions:

- show Harbor helping Theo
- show school staff trusting Harbor
- show Norah isolated by reasonableness
- show Theo hiding something small
- show first impossible or too-specific Harbor detail

### Act III

Needed scene functions:

- show records accumulating against Norah
- show Theo repeating Harbor language
- show Norah seeking proof
- show school meeting reframing her past
- show midpoint confirmation that Harbor knows too much

### Act IV

Needed scene functions:

- show institutional alignment
- show access or communication restriction
- show Theo realizing his feelings are being used as evidence
- show Harbor/Clasp lineage or data-source clue
- show Norah's low point and emotional shift toward trust

### Act V

Needed scene functions:

- assemble proof
- give Theo meaningful agency
- crack Harbor's frame
- force institutional response
- resolve Norah/Theo emotional question
- end with leaving as thematic action, not just plot escape
